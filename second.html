375 Reduction of Rents

The first land policy was the "375 Reduction of Rents." During Japan's rule over Taiwan, landlords who owned a significant amount of land had control over who could rent in a particular area, where demand was high but the land belonged to a small group of people. Tenants were required to give all their crops to the landlord, except for seed. Even in the event of natural disasters or crop failures, they still had to provide all their rent to the landlord. The rent was previously called "Mandatory Rentals," which meant that their rights were not protected, and they had to give up everything even if they did not have sufficient rights. The first land policy aimed to change this situation. The Republic of China government previously implemented a 50% rent policy in China, which meant that rent could not be higher than 50% of the crop. In April 1949, Taiwan Province President Chen Cheng announced a new land policy, "Reduction of Rents for Cultivated Land by 37.5%." It reduced 50% of the rent by 25%, resulting in the following formula:


50% x (1-0.25) = 37.5%

  1. Landlords may not charge rent in advance. If there is a crop failure, the rent should be even lower.
  2. The rental contract should be no less than six years.
  3. If the contract expires and the landlord does not intend to take it back for self-cultivation, it should be rented to the tenant.
  4. If the landlord plans to sell the land, the tenant has the right of first refusal.
  5. A rent association should be established in each town to handle land disputes or conflicts.


Public land sales and distribution

The following policy refers to the "sale of public land". According to the report of Taiwan's National Development Council, public land in Taiwan is owned by the Japanese government, private companies, and individuals. The land area is approximately 1,760 square kilometers. From 1951 to 1976, the Republic of China government conducted 9 transactions of public land sales. The land price was 2.5 times the annual rice production of the land. People had to pay the price for ten years through crops or cash. The purpose of this policy was to make people self-sufficient. This policy benefited 2.88627 million households, and the total area of land sold was 1,397,570,000 square meters.



Land Cultivation Program

In 1953, two years after the "sale of public land", the Republic of China government announced a new land policy, namely the "Land Cultivation Program". This policy allowed landlords to retain only a portion of their land, and then the government would take it away and distribute it to the people. Landlords received some compensation from the government, including shares of government-owned companies.
