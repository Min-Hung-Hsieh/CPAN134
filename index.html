Taiwan Introduction:


Taiwan is an island in the Pacific, and today it is home to different groups of people, including Fujian, Hakka, Chinese from other provinces, Taiwanese indigenous people, and Southeast Asian immigrants. Before World War II, Taiwan was a colony of Japan. In 1895, due to the failure of the First Sino-Japanese War, China ceded Taiwan, Liaodong Peninsula, and other areas to Japan. After World War II, the government of the Republic of China (ROC) moved to Taiwan and began building the island. When the ROC government arrived in Taiwan in 1949, nearly two million soldiers and their families also moved to Taiwan. At that time, there were many things that the ROC government needed to address.

When World War II ended in 1945, Taiwan's economic and health conditions were very low, and the ROC government had a significant impact on Taiwan's development by transforming the economic entity from agriculture to high-tech industries. From the 1960s to the 1980s, a series of serious new policies and development plans industrialized Taiwan. The National Statistics Bureau of Taiwan stated that in 1965, the per capita GDP was $229, and 30 years later, in 1984, the per capita GDP had increased to $3,225.

Do you know where Taiwan is?


Taiwan is a small island in the Pacific. It is adjacent to China, and the indigenous people on the island are considered to be the origin of the Austronesian language family in the Pacific islands. The land area of Taiwan is 13,972 square kilometers, and there are approximately 23.8 million people living on the island.
