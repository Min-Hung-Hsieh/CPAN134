Taiwan's Economic Takeoff

After the 1980s, the Taiwan government began implementing different economic policies, transforming the industrial structure from "heavy industry" to "technology-intensive industries". Technology-intensive industries include optoelectronics, computers, computer integrated manufacturing, and semiconductor industries, etc. Just like the processing zone before, the government established a special area for investors to build factories. This special area is called the "Science Park". Within the science park, there are many brands producing large quantities of semiconductor products. For example, TSMC, UMC, MediaTek, etc. The technology headquarters of these Taiwanese brands are all located within the Hsinchu Science Park. According to a report by the Liberty Times website last year, the total revenue of the science park exceeded 8.5 trillion US dollars in 2018. The semiconductor industry contributes significantly to Taiwan's GDP.
